Who We Are

SPIN Global is a public benefit company that exists to disrupt disasters from neighborhoods to nations. We aspire to be one of the world’s premier disaster consultancies, trusted to provide innovative solutions that tangibly and defensibly reduce risk and vulnerability to disasters.

Company Overview

As a management consulting firm, our approach is built on four key pillars:

  • Strategic Foresight.

    We listen to our client needs and provide navigational expertise to help them survive and thrive in uncertain conditions.

  • Public-Private Cooperation.

    We facilitate cooperation between private, public, and non-governmental organizations to connect unmet needs with resources.

  • Impact Innovation.

    We pioneer the creation of novel solutions that reduce disaster risk and vulnerability in the natural, social, built, economic, and cyber environments.

  • Neighborhoods and Nations.

    We tailor our approach to reflect the operating environment at any scale.

Why We Exist

We are moved by compassion to reduce physical, social, and economic suffering caused by disasters. We are committed to working together for a higher purpose with a spirit of excellence to create value for our clients and humanity while stewarding our people, planet, and profit. Our specific public benefits include:

  • Promoting the advancement of disaster literacy, risk and vulnerability awareness, risk reduction, and readiness to respond in neighborhoods and nations; and
  • Increasing the flow of capital to entities and projects that will reduce risk and vulnerability of people, infrastructure and systems.

Community Disaster Resilience Fund

The Community Disaster Resilience Fund exists to connect needs with resources available to develop disaster resistant infrastructure.

The Fund mobilizes private capital towards public benefit projects that significantly reduce exposure to risk, threats, and hazards while increasing economic opportunities. We are working with private and public entities to make generational investments in community resilience measured by their social and economic impacts.
